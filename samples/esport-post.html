The International 2023: Meta Analysis & Predictions

Dota 2 October 12, 2023 By Alex "Techies" Chen 42 Comments

The International 2023 promises to be the most competitive Dota 2 tournament yet

The biggest tournament in esports is upon us once again. The International 2023, with its $40 million prize pool, brings together the best Dota 2 teams in the world to battle for the Aegis of Champions. After an intense competitive season, we break down the meta, analyze the top contenders, and make our predictions for who will emerge victorious.

Dates

October 27 - November 5, 2023

Location

Singapore Indoor Stadium

Prize Pool

$40,018,195

Teams

20 (12 Direct Invites, 8 Qualifiers)

The Current Meta Breakdown

The 7.34d patch has solidified several key trends that will dominate The International:

  • Universal Heroes Dominance: With the universal attribute changes, heroes like Pangolier, Windranger, and Dark Seer have become top picks.
  • Early Game Aggression: Teams are prioritizing strong laning phases with heroes that can rotate early like Marci and Dawnbreaker.
  • Objective Focus: The changes to Tormentors and Watchers have made map control more important than ever.
  • Flexible Drafts: The most successful teams are those that can play multiple strategies and adapt mid-series.

"This might be the most balanced meta we've seen heading into a TI. There are at least 80 viable heroes, which means we'll see incredible diversity in drafts and strategies."

— Purge, Dota 2 Analyst

Dark Horses to Watch

While the favorites get most of the attention, these teams could make surprising deep runs:

  • BetBoom Team: The CIS squad has shown flashes of brilliance and could peak at the right time.
  • Shopify Rebellion: Arteezy's team finally looks cohesive and ready to challenge for a top finish.
  • Entity: The young European team plays with fearless aggression that could unsettle more experienced squads.

Predictions

After analyzing all the factors, here are our predictions for The International 2023:

  1. Gaimin Gladiators: Their consistency all year suggests they'll finally claim the Aegis.
  2. Team Spirit: The defending champions will push Gaimin to the limit in an epic finals.
  3. LGD Gaming: The Chinese powerhouse always performs at TI.
  4. Tundra Esports: The 2022 winners still have the strategic depth to challenge.

Regardless of who wins, The International 2023 promises to deliver unforgettable Dota action with the highest stakes ever.
